The Knowledge Base is your AI agent's reference library. Upload your company documents -- policies, FAQs, product guides, manuals -- and your agent will automatically use them to answer customer questions with accurate, up-to-date information.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your documents">
    Upload files through the dashboard or API. DEHA ONE supports PDF, Word, Excel, CSV, plain text, Markdown, and HTML files.
  </Step>
  <Step title="DEHA ONE processes them">
    Your documents are automatically broken into smaller sections, analyzed for meaning, and indexed so they can be searched quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your agent uses them">
    When a customer asks a question, your agent searches the knowledge base, finds the most relevant information, and uses it to craft an accurate response.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This all happens automatically. You do not need to tell your agent which document to look at or what to search for -- it figures that out on its own based on the customer's question.

## What You Can Upload

| Format | File Types | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Documents** | PDF, Word (.docx), PowerPoint (.pptx) | Includes scanned PDFs (OCR), tables, and figures |
| **Spreadsheets** | Excel (.xlsx), CSV | Sheet-aware parsing, schema inference |
| **Text files** | Plain text (.txt), Markdown (.md) | |
| **Web content** | HTML (.html), live URLs | The platform fetches and cleans the URL for you |
| **Structured data** | JSON (.json), JSONL (.jsonl) | Per-row vectorization with field selection |
| **Audio (optional)** | MP3, WAV, M4A | Transcribed via Whisper before indexing |

<Info>
  Parsing is powered by IBM Docling — it handles scanned PDFs (OCR), preserves tables, captures figures, and chunks text along document structure (headings, sections, paragraphs) rather than naive fixed-size chunks. The result: better recall when an agent searches.
</Info>

## Organizing with Collections

Collections let you group related documents together. Think of them as folders for your knowledge base. For example:

- **Company Policies** -- HR handbook, return policy, terms of service
- **Product Documentation** -- User guides, technical specs, release notes
- **FAQs** -- Common questions and answers

You can create as many collections as you need, or keep things simple with a single collection for all your documents.

<Tip>
  Start with one or two collections and add more as your knowledge base grows. You can always reorganize later.
</Tip>

## Connecting to Your Agents

To give an agent access to your knowledge base, enable it in the agent's settings. Once enabled, the agent will automatically search your documents when it needs information to answer a question.

You can also control which collections an agent can access. For example, your customer support agent might search "Product Documentation" and "FAQs", while your HR bot only searches "Company Policies".

## Your Data Stays Private

Every organization's knowledge base is completely isolated:

- Per-user Qdrant collections with **mandatory user-id filter** on every query
- **BYOD option**: point the knowledge base at your own Qdrant cluster — your data never sits on platform infrastructure
- **BYOK option**: use your own embedding API keys (OpenAI, Google, etc.) — embeddings are billed to your account, not platform credits
- Deterministic UUID5 deduplication — re-uploading the same content does not double-bill or duplicate vectors
- Zero-downtime re-indexing: when you switch embedding models, the platform does a blue-green migration and only flips reads once the new index is healthy

## Reranking for accuracy

For every query, the platform pulls the top-N candidates by vector similarity, then applies a **cross-encoder reranker** (`ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2`) to score them more precisely. This typically lifts top-1 accuracy by 15-30% compared to vector similarity alone. If the reranker is unavailable, the system falls back to vector similarity gracefully.

## Getting Started

1. **Upload a few documents** -- Start with your most frequently referenced materials (FAQs, product guides, policies)
2. **Create a collection** -- Organize your documents into at least one collection
3. **Enable knowledge base** on your agent -- Turn on knowledge base access in your agent's settings
4. **Test it** -- Ask your agent questions that require information from your uploaded documents
