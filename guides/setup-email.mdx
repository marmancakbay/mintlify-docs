## What You Will Set Up

Connect your email to DEHA ONE so that incoming customer emails are handled by your AI agent, which drafts and sends replies automatically.

<Info>
Before you begin, you need:
- An email service with SMTP credentials (e.g., SendGrid, AWS SES, or your own mail server)
- A DEHA ONE workspace with at least one [agent configured](/guides/first-agent)
- For inbound email: a domain with MX records you can configure, or a provider that supports webhook-based inbound parsing
</Info>

---

<Steps>

### Choose Your Email Provider

DEHA ONE works with any SMTP-compatible email service for sending replies and supports webhook-based inbound email processing.

| Provider | Recommended For |
|----------|----------------|
| **SendGrid** | Production use with inbound parse webhooks |
| **AWS SES** | Teams already on AWS |
| **Custom SMTP** | Any standard SMTP server |

### Add Email Credentials to DEHA ONE

1. In the DEHA ONE dashboard, go to **Settings > Vault**.
2. Add the following secrets:

| Secret Key | Example Value |
|-----------|--------------|
| `smtp_host` | `smtp.sendgrid.net` |
| `smtp_port` | `587` |
| `smtp_username` | `apikey` |
| `smtp_password` | `SG.xxxxx` |
| `smtp_from_email` | `support@yourcompany.com` |

### Enable the Email Channel

1. Go to **Settings > Channels** in the dashboard.
2. Enable **Email**.
3. Set the **default agent** for email conversations.
4. Click **Save**.

### Set Up Inbound Email

Configure your email provider to forward incoming emails to DEHA ONE.

**If using SendGrid Inbound Parse:**

1. Go to **SendGrid Settings > Inbound Parse**.
2. Add a new entry:
   - **Hostname:** Your subdomain (e.g., `inbound.yourcompany.com`)
   - **URL:** `https://api.deha.one/webhook/mail/{your_workspace_id}`
3. Add an MX record for the subdomain pointing to `mx.sendgrid.net`.

**If using another provider:**

Point your inbound email webhook to:
```
https://api.deha.one/webhook/mail/{your_workspace_id}
```

### Publish and Test

1. Go to **Settings > Configuration** and click **Publish**.
2. Send a test email to your configured inbound address.
3. Check the **Timeline** in the dashboard to see the email arrive and the agent's response.

<Check>
Send a test email and verify the agent processes it and sends a reply. Check both the dashboard timeline and your email inbox for the response.
</Check>

</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Outbound emails not being sent">
    Verify your SMTP credentials are correct in the vault. Make sure your `smtp_from_email` address is verified with your email provider.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inbound emails not arriving">
    Confirm your MX records are configured correctly for your subdomain. Check that the webhook URL is accessible from the internet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Emails marked as spam">
    Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records for your sending domain. Use a verified sender identity in your email provider.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
