## What You Will Build

Create a knowledge base by uploading your company documents. Your AI agent will search this knowledge base to give accurate, company-specific answers instead of relying on general knowledge.

<Info>
Before you begin, make sure you have:
- A DEHA ONE workspace with at least one [agent configured](/guides/first-agent)
- Documents to upload (PDF, Word, text files, spreadsheets, etc.)
</Info>

---

<Steps>

### How It Works

When a customer asks your agent a question, the agent searches your knowledge base for relevant information before answering. This is called **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** -- your agent retrieves real company information and uses it to generate accurate responses.

```
Customer asks: "What is your return policy?"
        |
  Agent searches your uploaded documents
        |
  Finds the relevant section about returns
        |
  Agent answers: "Our return policy allows returns within 30 days..."
```

### Create a Collection

Organize your documents into collections (like folders). For example, you might have separate collections for "Product Documentation," "Company Policies," and "FAQ."

1. Go to **Knowledge Base** in the dashboard.
2. Click **Create Collection**.
3. Name your collection (e.g., "Company Knowledge").
4. Choose an embedding model:
   - **Default (HuggingFace)** -- Free, good for general use
   - **OpenAI** -- Higher quality, requires an OpenAI API key
   - **Google** -- Good quality with Google Cloud integration

### Upload Documents

1. Open your collection.
2. Click **Upload Documents**.
3. Drag and drop files or click to browse.
4. DEHA ONE automatically processes your documents -- splitting them into searchable chunks and indexing them.

**Supported file formats:**

| Format | Extensions |
|--------|-----------|
| PDF | `.pdf` |
| Plain text | `.txt` |
| Markdown | `.md` |
| CSV | `.csv` |
| JSON | `.json` |
| YAML | `.yaml`, `.yml` |

<Tip>
You can also add knowledge by typing or pasting text directly. This is useful for FAQ entries, policy summaries, or any content that is not in a file.
</Tip>

### Add Metadata Tags

When uploading, you can tag documents with metadata (like category, department, or topic) to help organize and filter search results.

For example, tag a document with `category: policies` and `department: hr` so the agent can find it more easily when answering HR policy questions.

### Test Your Knowledge Base

Before connecting it to your agent, test that search works correctly:

1. Go to your collection in the dashboard.
2. Use the **Search** box to try a question.
3. Check that relevant document chunks appear in the results.
4. Verify the relevance scores -- higher scores mean better matches.

**Example searches to try:**
- "What is the return policy?"
- "How do I reset my password?"
- "What are the business hours?"

### Connect to Your Agent

Once your knowledge base is populated, make sure your agent is set up to use it:

1. Go to **Agents** and select your agent.
2. In the agent settings, make sure the **Knowledge Search** tool is enabled.
3. In the system prompt, instruct the agent to search the knowledge base:

```
Always search the knowledge base before answering questions about
company policies, products, or procedures. If you cannot find relevant
information, let the customer know honestly.
```

4. **Publish** your configuration.

<Check>
Ask your agent a question that can be answered from your uploaded documents. Verify that it searches the knowledge base and returns accurate information from your content.
</Check>

### Keep Your Knowledge Base Updated

As your company information changes, keep your knowledge base current:

- **Upload new documents** when policies or products change.
- **Remove outdated content** from collections.
- **Re-upload updated versions** of existing documents -- the system handles deduplication automatically.

</Steps>

---

## Tips for a Great Knowledge Base

- **Cover common questions first.** Upload the documents that answer your most frequent customer questions.
- **Use clear, well-structured documents.** Documents with headings and organized sections produce better search results.
- **Keep documents focused.** Several focused documents work better than one massive document covering everything.
- **Test with real customer questions.** Search for the actual questions your customers ask to verify coverage.
