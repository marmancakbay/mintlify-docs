DEHA ONE uses role-based access to manage what each team member can do on the platform. Every user is assigned one of five roles, each with a clear set of permissions.

---

## Available roles

### Owner

Full control over the organization. Owners can manage billing, invite and remove users, configure agents, deploy changes, and access all platform features. Every organization has at least one Owner.

### Admin

Full operational access. Admins can create and edit configurations, deploy changes to production, test agents, and manage day-to-day operations. Admins cannot manage billing.

### Supervisor

Supervisors can view and edit configurations -- agents, pipelines, channels, and data sources -- but they cannot deploy changes to production. This provides a natural review layer: Supervisors prepare changes, and Admins or Owners deploy them.

### Agent

Agents are your frontline team members. They can view dashboards, handle customer conversations, and respond to human review requests from AI agents. They cannot modify configurations.

### Viewer

Read-only access to dashboards, analytics, and reports. Viewers cannot modify anything on the platform. Use this role for managers, executives, or external partners who need visibility without edit access.

| Permission | Owner | Admin | Supervisor | Agent | Viewer |

| View dashboards and reports | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |

## Managing your team

### Inviting new users

Owners and Admins can invite new team members:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Team**
2. Click **Invite Member**
3. Enter their email address
4. Select the appropriate role
5. Send the invitation

The new member will receive an email with instructions to sign in via SSO.

### Changing a user's role

To change an existing team member's role:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Team**
2. Find the team member
3. Click their current role to change it
4. Select the new role

<Info>
  The same person can have different roles in different organizations. For example, someone might be an Admin in one organization and a Viewer in another.
</Info>

### Removing a user

To remove a team member:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Team**
2. Find the team member
3. Click **Remove**

Their access is revoked immediately.

## Human review permissions

When an AI agent flags a response for human review, the review request may specify a minimum role level. For example, a sensitive approval might require a Supervisor or above, while a routine review could be handled by any Agent.

| Required level | Who can respond |
|---------------|----------------|
| Agent | Agent, Supervisor, Admin, Owner |
| Supervisor | Supervisor, Admin, Owner |
| Admin | Admin, Owner |
| Owner | Owner only |

## Best practices

- **Follow least privilege.** Assign each team member the minimum role they need. Most team members should be Agents or Viewers.
- **Limit Owner and Admin roles.** Only assign these to people who genuinely need full access.
- **Use Supervisor for team leads.** Supervisors can prepare configuration changes without the risk of deploying untested changes to production.
- **Use Viewer for stakeholders.** Give read-only access to managers and partners who need to see dashboards without the ability to change anything.
- **Review roles regularly.** Periodically check your team list and remove or downgrade access for people who no longer need it.
