DEHA is the AI assistant at the heart of DEHA ONE. Instead of navigating complex settings or writing code, you simply tell DEHA what you want in plain language. It understands your business needs, builds the solution, tests it, and makes sure everything works -- all in a single conversation.

**Think of DEHA as your personal platform engineer.** It can create customer support bots, set up data pipelines, connect your tools, and design multi-agent workflows -- without you ever touching a line of code.

## How DEHA is built

Under the hood, DEHA is a **multi-agent team** orchestrated by a single brain.

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| **Cortex** | The router and brain. Parses intent, decomposes the request, picks which sub-agent(s) to engage, and synthesizes the final reply. |
| **Commander** | Handles configuration changes: create / update / delete agents, pipelines, routes, channels, vault entries. Validates against schemas, applies atomically. |
| **Analyst** | Handles data queries: text-to-SQL, profiling, ML, anomaly, forecasting -- anything that reads or computes over your data. |
| **Communicator** | Handles platform Q&A: feature explanations, credit usage, costs, status, what-do-I-have. |
| **Smart Healer** | Owns its own LLM call. When tests fail, applies repair strategies (rephrase prompts, adjust tools, split steps, retry with backoff, etc.) and remembers what worked. |
| **Judge** | Scores every response on 6 dimensions: relevance, safety, accuracy, compliance, performance, bias. |

You only ever talk to one assistant. The orchestration is invisible.

## See DEHA in action

Here is what a typical conversation with DEHA looks like:

<Steps>
  <Step title="You describe what you need">
    Tell DEHA what you want to build in your own words. No special syntax required.

    > **You:** "I need a customer support bot for our WhatsApp channel. It should be able to look up order status using our Shopify API and escalate to a human agent when the customer is frustrated."
  </Step>
  <Step title="DEHA presents a plan">
    DEHA analyzes your request, considers your existing setup, and creates a clear plan.

    > **DEHA:** "Here's what I'll build for you:
    >
    > 1. A customer support agent connected to your WhatsApp channel
    > 2. Integration with your Shopify API for order lookups
    > 3. Automatic escalation to your support team when frustration is detected
    >
    > Risk: Low -- this creates new components without affecting existing ones.
    >
    > Shall I proceed?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="You approve (or adjust)">
    Review the plan and give the green light, or ask for changes before execution.

    > **You:** "Looks good, but also add the ability to process returns."
    >
    > **DEHA:** "Updated the plan to include return processing via Shopify. Ready to proceed?"
    >
    > **You:** "Yes, go ahead."
  </Step>
  <Step title="DEHA builds, tests, and delivers">
    DEHA creates your solution, runs automated tests, and confirms everything works.

    > **DEHA:** "All done! Your customer support bot is live on WhatsApp. Here's what I built:
    >
    > - Support agent with order lookup and return processing
    > - Shopify API integration (order status + returns)
    > - Automatic escalation when frustration is detected
    >
    > I tested the agent with sample conversations and everything passed. Your customers can start messaging now."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How DEHA keeps things safe

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Always shows a plan first">
    DEHA never makes changes without your approval. Every request produces a clear plan with a summary, risk assessment, and rollback strategy. You decide when to proceed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Automatic testing">
    After building your solution, DEHA runs automated tests to verify everything works correctly. You get a clear pass or fail result with details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Self-healing">
    If a test reveals an issue, DEHA automatically diagnoses the problem and attempts to fix it. You are notified of any fixes made and their outcomes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Version history and rollback">
    Every change is versioned. If you ever need to go back, DEHA can roll back to any previous version of your configuration.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Destructive actions require confirmation">
    Deleting agents, pipelines, or integrations always requires your explicit confirmation -- even if you have auto-execution enabled.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
