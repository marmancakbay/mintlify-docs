DEHA ONE processes your data, but it does not want to own it. Two features make that practical:

1. **Versioning** — every snapshot is point-in-time, time-travelable, and rollback-able
2. **BYOD (Bring Your Own Data)** — results sink back to your own storage, with the platform keeping only operational state

---

## Versioning (Delta Lake snapshots)

A **snapshot** is the state of a named dataset at a specific point in time. Snapshots are immutable, content-addressable, and stored in Delta Lake format.

### What you get

| Capability | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Append a new snapshot** | Roll forward to a new version with a single pipeline step |
| **Time-travel read** | Query any past version by name + version number or by timestamp |
| **Rollback** | Re-point the "current" pointer to any past version atomically |
| **Diff** | Row-level diff between any two versions (added / removed / changed) |
| **Schema evolution** | Tracked across versions; backward-compatible reads |
| **Retention** | Configurable per dataset (default 30 days of versions) |

### When to snapshot

- After a daily / hourly load — so you can roll back if downstream complains
- Before a destructive transform — so the "before" state survives
- Before pushing to production — so the prior production state is preserved
- At quarter close — for audit and compliance evidence

### Rollback in practice

If a downstream report looks wrong, ask DEHA:

> "Roll the customer_master dataset back to yesterday at 18:00 and re-run the weekly KPI pipeline."

DEHA validates the request, performs the atomic re-point (no data movement), and re-runs the dependent pipelines. Total time: seconds.

## BYOD — Bring Your Own Data

DEHA ONE's BYOD model means **your business data lives in your warehouse, not ours**. The platform processes data ephemerally and sinks the results back to systems you own.

### BYOD destinations

| Destination | What you can sink to it |
|---|---|
| **PostgreSQL** | Tables, materialized views |
| **MySQL** | Tables |
| **MongoDB** | Collections (per-user prefix preserved) |
| **MSSQL / Oracle / SQLite** | Tables |
| **S3 (any S3-compatible)** | Parquet / CSV / JSON object snapshots |
| **External Qdrant** | Vector collections |
| **External MongoDB** | Archive of platform events |
| **REST webhook** | Push-based downstream notification |

### Storage strategies per user

Pick how data flows for your user:

- **INTERNAL** — operational state only; business data may not flow to platform storage
- **EXTERNAL** — everything routes to your storage (warehouse, Mongo, Qdrant)
- **HYBRID** — operational state internal, business data external (most common)

Per-data-category routing is supported: `BUSINESS_DATA`, `ANALYTICS_RESULTS`, `EMBEDDINGS`, `FILES`, `CONVERSATIONS` can each go to a different destination.

### Why BYOD matters

- **Data residency** — keep data in your region of choice
- **Existing investments** — leverage your warehouse, your Mongo, your Qdrant, your S3
- **Cost control** — pay for storage in your provider's terms, not the platform's
- **Compliance** — your DLP, your encryption keys, your access policies
- **Vendor lock-in mitigation** — your data is always in your systems, not the platform's

## Operational vs. business data

| Data category | Where it lives |
|---|---|
| **Operational** (sessions, locks, idempotency keys, lineage) | Platform |
| **Business data** (your tables, your customers, your transactions) | You (BYOD) or platform (your choice) |
| **Analytics results** | You (BYOD) or platform |
| **Conversations / messages** | You (BYOD) or platform |
| **Embeddings** | You (BYOD Qdrant) or platform Qdrant |
| **Files / attachments** | You (BYOD S3) or platform |

You decide which category goes where on a per-user basis.

## DuckDB inside the platform

For transient analytics work — text-to-SQL, profiling, ad-hoc queries — DEHA ONE uses a per-user DuckDB database. DuckDB is:

- File-isolated per user
- Read-only for analytics / query workloads
- Ephemeral by default (process and discard)
- Optionally persistent with TTL-based sink to your BYOD destination

DuckDB connects directly to your warehouse via native scanners (PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB), so queries against your live warehouse work without moving data into the platform.

## Audit and lineage

Every snapshot and every BYOD write produces a lineage event in **OpenLineage** format, forwarded to **Marquez** (or your own lineage system). You can:

- Trace any downstream artifact back to every input that produced it
- See which pipeline / agent / user triggered the write
- Browse the DAG of your data ecosystem
- Audit cross-user data movement (and prove there is none)
