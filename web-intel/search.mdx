DEHA ONE's search service fans your query out to 13 providers in parallel, deduplicates URLs, and re-scores results so the most relevant ones float to the top.

---

## The 13 providers

| Provider | Best for | Free tier? |
|---|---|---|
| **Tavily** | AI-native general web search optimized for LLM context | 1K queries/month |
| **GDELT** | News and events across 65+ languages, ~3-month window | Free |
| **Brave Search** | Independent, spam-filtered general web index | Free with key |
| **SerpAPI - Google** | Organic Google SERP results | Paid (cheapest tier OK) |
| **SerpAPI - Google News** | Fresh news with date filtering | Paid |
| **SerpAPI - Google Places** | Local business listings (no reviews) | Paid |
| **SerpAPI - Google Reviews** | Reviews for a single place | Paid |
| **SerpAPI - Google Shopping** | Product listings with pricing | Paid |
| **SerpAPI - Google Trends** | Time-series interest data + related queries | Paid |
| **Twitter (TwitterAPI.io)** | Tweets with engagement metadata | Paid |
| **YouTube (SerpAPI)** | Video listings | Paid |
| **YouTube Data API** | Views, likes, captions, channel info | 10K units/day free |
| **Sikayetvar** | Turkish complaint platform (scraped) | Free |

You only enable the providers you have keys for. The platform skips any provider whose key is missing and proceeds with the rest.

## What a search returns

Every result, regardless of provider, comes back in a unified schema:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `url` | The deduplicated URL (trailing slash and fragments stripped) |
| `title` | Page title |
| `snippet` | Provider-supplied excerpt |
| `provider` | Which provider returned it |
| `provider_score` | The raw score the provider gave |
| `published_at` | When the page was published (when known) |
| `relevance_score` | The platform's reranked score (0..1) |

The reranker blends three signals:

- **40%** provider score
- **30%** keyword match against the query
- **30%** recency (newer = higher)

You can adjust the weights or disable the rerank if you want raw provider output.

## Caching

Search results are cached for 30 minutes per `(user, query, provider set)` tuple. Repeated queries within the cache window do not consume credits.

---

## Monitor mode

Long-running monitors run a query on a schedule (typically every 15 minutes to a few hours) and emit a CloudEvent whenever **new URLs** appear -- not seen in any previous run.

Monitors are cheaper (2 credits each) and ideal for:

- Brand mention tracking
- Competitor watch
- Regulatory and news alerts
- Outage detection from social signals

Pair a monitor with a [scheduled pipeline](/automations/scheduling) and a [Slack channel](/channels/slack) to get notifications without polling code.

## Example: brand watch every morning

```yaml
pipeline: brand-watch-morning
trigger:
  type: cron
  expression: "0 8 * * *"

steps:
  - type: web_discover
    query: '"DEHA ONE" OR "deha.one"'
    providers: [tavily, google_news, twitter, sikayetvar]
    max_per_provider: 10

  - type: web_scrape
    urls: ${previous.urls}
    max_urls: 5
    mode: markdown

  - type: llm
    prompt: |
      Summarize the following mentions in 5 bullets, flag anything that
      looks like a complaint, and identify any factual errors.
    input: ${previous.results}

  - type: publish
    channel: slack
    target: "#brand-watch"
    text: ${previous.summary}
```

DEHA can generate exactly this pipeline from one sentence. Once deployed, it runs every morning, costs a handful of credits, and surfaces real signal without you doing anything.

## Bring your own keys

Provider keys are vaulted per user. You can:

- Use your own keys (recommended for production -- your quota, your billing)
- Use platform-provided keys for prototyping (subject to platform rate limits)
- Mix and match: e.g. your own SerpAPI key but platform Tavily for spillover

See [Security & Vault](/security/byok-and-vault) for how vaulting works.
